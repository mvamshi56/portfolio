A few years ago, I was deep in the rabbit hole of trying to find the single "best" AI model. ChatGPT was dominating headlines, so surely it should do everything, right?

Wrong.

Here's what I learned the hard way: **every AI model has a specialty.**

I now run 6 different models in my daily workflow:

- **Claude** — for long-form writing and content strategy. Its reasoning depth is unmatched for complex marketing copy.
- **ChatGPT** — quick drafts, brainstorming, and code snippets. Fast and versatile.
- **Gemini** — SEO topic clustering and keyword analysis. Natively handles large context windows of search data.
- **Custom TypeScript agents** — automated SEO audits, crawling, and structured data extraction.
- **GitHub Copilot** — writing marketing automation scripts in Python and Node.js.
- **Midjourney** — visual concepts for landing pages and ad creatives.

Each one does one thing well. None of them does everything.

The real skill isn't knowing which model is "best" — it's knowing **which model to reach for, and when.** That's the difference between someone who uses AI and someone who actually gets results from it.
