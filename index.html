About Me

From QA Engineer to AI Marketing Leader

I spent my early career as a Software Test Engineer. Doesn't sound like marketing, right? But here's the thing. Testing taught me how to break things down, find the root cause, and fix it methodically. Eight years later, that analytical habit is still how I approach every campaign and every strategy.

When I moved into digital marketing around 2015, it clicked immediately. SEO audits, campaign analysis, A/B testing. Same muscle, different domain. Over the years I've managed Google Ads budgets north of Rs.2L/month, built SEO strategies that delivered real growth, and started building AI tools when I realized how much busywork could be automated away. Every role I took taught me something new — from managing ecommerce campaigns to running content strategies for B2B SaaS companies.

So I taught myself to code. TypeScript, Python, Node.js. Built an AI SEO audit crawler, a Chrome extension for web summarization, and a security analysis platform. They're not portfolio pieces — I use them in my actual workflow. That shift from using tools to building them changed how I think about marketing automation completely. I went from waiting on developers to shipping my own solutions.

My edge is simple: I speak both marketing and engineering. I can sit with the CMO to plan the strategy, then go build the tool that executes it. That crossover is still uncommon, and it's what lets me deliver work that's both creative and technically sound. I don't just run reports — I build the pipelines that generate them.

Right now I'm focused on combining my marketing experience with AI automation. Building tools that save hours of manual work, running campaigns that actually convert, and helping businesses grow without the bloated agency overhead. I work best when there's a real problem to solve and a clear way to measure success.

SEO AI

Recovering a Client's Organic Traffic After a Core Update

A B2B SaaS site lost 40% of its organic traffic after Google's March 2024 core update. We used automated crawling and NLP to identify and fix 200+ issues.

Problem

A client's website — a B2B SaaS platform with ~500 pages — saw organic traffic drop from ~8,000 visits/month to ~4,800 over 3 months after a core algorithm update. Manual SEO audit would have taken 2+ weeks, and with a limited monthly retainer, they couldn't afford that kind of time. The drop was likely a mix of thin content, broken internal links, and outdated schema.

Strategy

I ran the site through a custom SEO crawler I built using TypeScript and Playwright — it checks on-page factors, internal link health, meta quality, and page speed in a single pass. It flagged 230+ issues ranked by estimated impact. I fixed the high-priority items first: 404s, orphaned pages, missing meta descriptions on 60+ pages, and rewritten thin content clusters using NLP topic suggestions from the same tool.

Result

15% traffic recovery in 3 months (~5,500 visits/month). Audit time dropped from ~2 weeks of manual work to about 6 hours using the automated crawler. 70+ leads/month from organic. The client kept using the tool for quarterly health checks.

Organic Traffic4.8K → 5.5K visits/mo
Audit Time2 Weeks → 6 Hours
PPC AI

Google Ads Restructuring for a Software Services Company

A software services firm was spending ~Rs.2L/month on Google Ads with a rising CPL. We consolidated campaigns and introduced automated bid rules.

Problem

A software services company was running Google Search Ads across 40+ ad groups with a monthly budget of ~Rs.2.2L. Cost-per-lead had climbed from ~Rs.850 to ~Rs.1,100 over two quarters — eating into margins. Their manual bid adjustment process meant checking campaign performance once a week and tweaking bids, which was too slow for auction dynamics. GA4 was set up but conversion data wasn't feeding into bid strategies effectively.

Strategy

Consolidated 40+ loosely organized ad groups into 12 service-line campaigns with clear theme alignment. Set up automated bid rules using target CPA with real-time conversion data from GA4. Built a Google Sheet script that pulled daily Ads performance via API and flagged keywords drifting above target CPA. Launched responsive search ads with systematic A/B testing — paused underperforming variants after 2-week cycles.

Result

CPL dropped from ~Rs.1,100 to ~Rs.770 over 8 weeks without reducing lead volume. ROAS improved from ~3.2x to ~5.5x. The daily reporting script saved roughly 6 hours/week of manual data work for the team. Budget allocation shifted to better-performing campaigns, improving overall return.

Cost Per LeadRs.1,100 → Rs.770
ROAS3.2x → 5.5x
Reporting Time~6 hrs/wk → Automated
Content AI

Building an AI-Assisted Content Pipeline for a Marketing Team

A small in-house marketing team was struggling to publish more than 4–5 SEO-optimized articles per month. We built an AI-assisted workflow that doubled their output without adding headcount.

Problem

The marketing team at a mid-sized SaaS company had one writer and one SEO specialist. They were publishing 4–5 blog posts/month, but competitors were publishing 15–20. The bottleneck wasn't writing — it was research: finding topics, outlining, keyword mapping, and optimizing drafts. Each article took ~5 days from idea to publish.

Strategy

I set up a structured content pipeline using Gemini for topic clustering and brief generation. The writer would feed a seed keyword, and the AI would suggest related topics, search volume estimates, and outline structures. We used custom prompts tuned to the brand voice for first drafts. The SEO specialist reviewed and optimized before publish — no AI-generated content went live without human review. This wasn't about replacing the writer; it was about removing research overhead.

Result

Output went from ~4 articles/month to 15–18 articles/month within 6 weeks. Average time-to-publish dropped from 5 days to ~1.5 days. Organic traffic from blog content grew ~25% over 4 months. The writer reported spending less time on research and more on actual writing and editing.

Monthly Output4 → 15-18 articles
Time to Publish5 Days → 1.5 Days
Blog Traffic+25% in 4 months
